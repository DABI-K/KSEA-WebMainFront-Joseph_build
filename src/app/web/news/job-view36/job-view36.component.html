KSEA Job Posting - KSEA Information Technology Staff

KSEA Information Technology Staff-Full Time
Job Description

Position Description

Korean-American Scientists and Engineers Association (KSEA) is seeking a creative Information Technology (IT) Staff with the emphasis on Full Stack Web Systems Development. If you are seeking an opportunity to play a significant role in helping a non-profit organization while also earning income and enhancing your resume, this is the job for you.

The KSEA IT Staff reports to IT Directors and Admin Directors. KSEA IT Staff is accountable for a quality IT experience by providing visionary technical leadership and information systems management to ensure the success of the KSEA operations and events management. The KSEA IT Staff leads and manages all websites and information systems for the businesses of its headquarter and local chapters/branches. This position supports the IT-related demands of headquarter staff members, and fosters cybersecurity and information assurance awareness. This position also actively engages in IT projects under the IT Directors' supervision. This position is a 3-month contract with the potential to convert full-time (or part-time) employees under annual renewal.


About the Korean-American Scientists and Engineers Association (KSEA)

KSEA was established in 1971 as a non-profit professional organization and has grown to over 8,000 registered members with ~ 70 Local Chapter and Branches, ~30 Affiliated Professional Societies, and 13 Technical Groups across the United States. The organization's objectives are to promote the application of science and technology for the general welfare of society, foster international cooperation, especially between the U.S. and Korea, and help Korean-American Scientists and Engineers develop their full career potential. Details about KSEA are available from the university's website at https://www.ksea.org/.


Essential Duties and Responsibilities are listed below. Other duties may be assigned.

  1. Support all IT-related businesses of KSEA, including HQ Staff, Executive Committee, Directors Committee, and Local Chapters.
  2. Design, develop, modify, debug, and evaluate all KSEA information systems, including websites, web applications, mobile apps, software applications, and databases.
  3. Plan, develop, and update web application architecture to be extensible.
  4. Create holistic web and mobile solutions to address short and long-term business goals.
  5. Maintain IT and IS currency and lead the effort to review and revise plans to enhance the KSEA member experience.
  6. Seek excellence in administering and modernizing KSEA IT and IS.
  7. Oversee and monitor the performance of IT and IS to ensure KSEA operation quality.
  8. Develop and advocate for new IT business development and IT-related staff development.
  9. Ensure that the KSEA IT is in alignment with the KSEA's business and growth plans.
  10. Keep lifelong learning to integrate emerging information technologies and computing paradigms into KSEA IT and IS.
  11. Effective communication and teamwork abilities.
  12. Excellent problem-solving skills and attention to detail.
