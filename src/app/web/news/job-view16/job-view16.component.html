Korean-American Scientists and Engineers Association (KSEA)

KIC DC Tech Frontier 프로그램 인턴모집

April 18, 2023

Korea Innovation Center Washington, D.C. (KIC DC) is currently looking for multiple Korean-English bilingual interns for the upcoming Tech Frontier Program. As an intern, you will have exposure to various elements of startup modules such as business model canvas, business networking, customer discovery, and business verification.

About KIC:
KIC DC is funded by the Korean government on a mission to create a global startup ecosystem throughout our four locations- Washington D.C., Silicon Valley, Berlin, and Beijing. We discover and incubate promising Korean technology startups by providing assets such as connections to market enablers, accelerators, and training programs. More than 100 Korean startups join us to explore the U.S market annually. We welcome leaders from various sectors to build a vibrant network between the U.S. and South Korea.

Job Title:
Tech Frontier Internship

Job Types:
Part-time, Internship

Job Summary:
Interns will be helping with multiple weeks of intensive Lean Startup entrepreneurial training and technology commercialization boot camps for teams of Korean researchers selected by the KIC DC for the Tech Frontier Program this summer. This will be a great opportunity for interns to engage with some of the best entrepreneurs/startups from Korea who are coming from prestigious universities in Korea. Interns will work closely with university-based Korean startup teams under the leadership of the George Washington Office of Innovation and Entrepreneurship and University of California, Berkeley Haas Entrepreneurship.

Locations:
Washington D.C. OR San Francisco

Term of internship:

Cohort 1 and 2 (Washington D.C.)

  • Virtual: 2nd OR 3rd week of July (TBD)
  • In-person: Jul 24th ~ Aug 11th, 2023
  • Field trips: TBD

Cohort 3 (San Francisco)

  • Virtual: 2nd OR 3rd week of July (TBD)
  • In-person: Jul 24th ~ Aug 11th, 2023
  • Field trips: TBD

Job Responsibilities:

  • Work about 20 hours per week
    • - More hours upon request from Korean startup teams
  • Attend internship orientation prior to cohort
  • Attend virtual training sessions prior to cohort
  • Work with the assigned teams outside of class to:
    • - Provide logistical help to the Korean entrepreneur groups and startup teams
    • - Assist teams with U.S market research and customer discovery interviews
    • - Other necessary help to the Korean start up teams via zoom or in-person upon request
  • Provide translation between Korean and English (& vice versa)
  • Support program logistics/coordination as requested
    • - Engage with the teaching team and KIC DC
    • - Special projects/tasks as assigned

Basic Qualifications:

  • Must be bilingual (fluent in both Korean & English)
